# Coinage — Three Views Canonical Spec

## 1. Purpose
Coinage needs three distinct views to keep strategy readable across scales without collapsing gameplay into a single overloaded interface. The player must always understand whether they are acting at world scale, faction-territory scale, or city-management scale.

## 2. Core Principle
Coinage reuses the **Grepolis structural hierarchy** as a product truth:
- **Map View** (macro world)
- **Faction View** (local territory)
- **City View** (internal management)

What changes is the artistic direction, not the structural reading logic. Coinage keeps Grepolis-like spatial comprehension while applying a dark premium techno military-command-center visual identity.

## 3. Map View
### Purpose
The Map View is the primary strategic surface of the game.

### What is visible
- The world as navigable spatial space
- Faction-islands distributed across a digital ocean
- City slots directly visible on each faction-island
- Free/occupied slot readability at map scale
- Clusters, void, and distance relationships

### What the player must understand immediately
- Where territories are located
- Where playable capacity exists (city slots)
- Which areas are dense, isolated, exposed, or strategic

### Navigation role
Map View is the entry point for spatial decision-making and the canonical source of world awareness.

### Why this is the primary view
Coinage is a territorial strategy game. The map is not a support panel; it is the game surface where strategic reading begins.

## 4. Faction View
### Purpose
Faction View is the local, zoomed-in spatial view of a single faction-island.

### Why it is a local spatial view
It is the layer where map-level readability becomes local territorial readability: slot arrangement, occupancy clarity, and local structure.

### What becomes clearer than on Map View
- Exact slot distribution
- Clear free vs occupied slot status
- Local positional relationships inside the faction-island

### What must remain spatial
- Territory shape and internal geography cues
- Slot positions as part of place, not detached metadata
- Local movement/selection context

### What must be avoided
- Turning Faction View into a detail sheet
- Card-heavy dashboard composition
- Replacing territory with abstract lists

## 5. City View
### Purpose
City View is the internal management layer for one city.

### Management role
This is where players handle city-level systems (construction, economy, military preparation, and city operations).

### What can be more UI-heavy
City View can use denser UI, more controls, and more transactional information than Map/Faction views.

### What must still feel like a game
Even with higher UI density, City View must preserve strategic identity and visual coherence with the world. It must not look like a generic admin console.

## 6. Navigation Between Views
Canonical flow:
- **Map → Faction → City**

Mental scale shift:
1. **Map**: global orientation and strategic positioning
2. **Faction**: local territorial inspection and slot-level understanding
3. **City**: internal execution and optimization

Returning upward (City → Faction → Map) must preserve spatial continuity so the player never loses world context.

## 7. Information Hierarchy
- **Map View (macro)**: world distribution, faction-island masses, city slot presence, cluster/void structure, strategic distance
- **Faction View (local)**: precise slot readability, local occupancy state, local territorial composition
- **City View (micro)**: management systems, progression controls, operational details

Rule: information belongs to the highest level where it is still readable and actionable without breaking spatial logic.

## 8. Anti-Patterns
Forbidden directions:
- Dashboard-first logic replacing spatial reading
- Faction View as a sheet/list page
- Graph-like or node-like world map in place of territory masses
- City View as an enterprise admin panel
- Any transition model that breaks spatial continuity between Map, Faction, and City
